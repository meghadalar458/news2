India, Australia to co-lead talks on climate finance at COP27

Climate finance is one of the main issues of contention at COP27 and India is among the countries that have highlighted the gap in delivery of climate finance from developed nations

Union minister for environment, forest & climate change, Bhupender Yadav at the UN Climate Summit, COP27. (AP)

India and Australia will co-lead ministerial consultations on key outstanding issues for climate finance over the next three days at UN climate conference, COP27 in Sharm el-Sheikh, Egypt, according to members of the Indian delegation.

Climate finance is one of the main issues of contention at COP27, cutting across finance for mitigation, adaptation and loss and damage. India is also among the countries that have highlighted the gap in delivery of climate finance from developed nations. It has also sought a multilaterally agreed definition of climate finance.
